# Safety Policy

POSA is safety-sensitive software. The app may be used in stressful outdoor or emergency contexts, so it must avoid unsupported certainty.

## Product Safety Rules

- Show sources and provenance.
- Separate source excerpts from synthesis.
- Mark draft and unreviewed content.
- Never treat user-imported packs as official.
- Do not present generated content as guaranteed true.
- Do not provide medical treatment guidance in v0.
- Do not hide uncertainty.
- Do not require network access for core field use.

## Required User-Facing Boundaries

The app should communicate that:

- POSA is not a substitute for emergency services.
- POSA is not medical, legal, rescue, or professional advice.
- Map and location data can be inaccurate or outdated.
- Users are responsible for field judgment and local conditions.
- When emergency services are available, users should contact them.

## AI And Retrieval Safety

Before any generated answer feature ships:

- Retrieval must work without generation.
- Answers must cite installed sources.
- The app must support "I do not know from installed sources."
- Medical treatment generation must remain blocked unless a separate reviewed policy exists.
- Gear and map context must be presented as context, not certainty.

## Public Alpha Label

Early releases should be labeled alpha or beta until field testing, review, and safety policies mature.
