## Field Use

- Carry a tent, tarp, bivy, emergency blanket, or other trip-appropriate shelter.
- Put insulation between you and cold or wet ground when possible.
- Use natural windbreaks only when they do not add hazards.
- Keep rain gear and shelter reachable before weather changes.
- Bright clothing or gear can make a shelter location easier to spot.

## Watchpoints

- Drainages, dry creek beds, and low spots can become unsafe in storms.
- Exposed ridges may be colder, windier, and more visible to lightning risk.
- A quick, modest shelter can be better than spending too much daylight building.
