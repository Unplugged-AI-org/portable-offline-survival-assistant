## Field Use

- Carry at least two ignition options in water-resistant storage.
- Bring a small fire starter that catches quickly and sustains flame.
- Check local fire restrictions before the trip and again at the trailhead when posted.
- Prefer a stove or no-flame option when fire danger is elevated.
- Keep ignition supplies accessible, not buried at the bottom of the pack.

## Watchpoints

- Wind, dry grass, duff, roots, and overhanging branches raise fire risk.
- A fire that is useful for warmth or signaling can also create a new emergency.
- Leave the site cold and clean if a lawful fire is used.
