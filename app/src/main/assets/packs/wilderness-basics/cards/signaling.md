## Field Use

- Stay near a known location if moving would make your position less certain.
- Put bright gear where it can be seen from open ground or air without creating a new hazard.
- Use a whistle, light, mirror, or bright clothing as conditions allow.
- Keep signals deliberate and repeatable instead of using all battery or energy at once.
- Mark your current location and direction of any short necessary movement.

## Watchpoints

- Thick forest, terrain folds, weather, and darkness can hide visual signals.
- A smoky or fiery signal can create unacceptable wildfire risk.
- Conserve phone and light power for location checks, contact attempts, and night visibility.
