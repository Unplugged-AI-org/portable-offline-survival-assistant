## Field Use

- Study the route, terrain, return path, and bail-out options before departure.
- Tell a reliable contact where you are going, your route, vehicle location, group size, and expected return time.
- Carry a map and compass, and know how to use them before the trip.
- Download offline maps before leaving service and bring backup power.
- If you may be lost, stop moving long enough to think, observe, and make a deliberate plan.

## Watchpoints

- Do not walk aimlessly to "see what is ahead" when you lack a position fix.
- Nightfall, exhaustion, injury, or low confidence are reasons to stay put and become easier to find.
- A drainage or stream can be dangerous terrain; treat it as a last-resort navigation clue, not a default route.
